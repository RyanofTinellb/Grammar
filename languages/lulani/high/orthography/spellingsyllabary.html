Spelling Syllabary

There is a spelling syllabary, used for transmitting words across potentially noisy communication channels, such as telephone lines. It is also used for communicaing in situations where every syllable is important, such as giving names. Each syllable corresponds to a disyllabic word, which begins with the same consonant and ends with its vowel. The geminate symbol is represented by the word faʔa へっあ language.
