Gerunds

The gerund suffix -ru る changes basic verbs into nouns describing the activity.

    • hubaru ほばる breathing
    • malaru まわる reflecting
    • mikuru みくる being red

Compound verbs, as well as those involving noun incorporation, can also be changed like this.

    • dissu’ahatiru でっすあはてる feeling happy
    • pakicuru ぱきゆる believing to be true
    • ’i’issiru いいっせる singing

The genitive determiners, including -qa げ, allow for (only) the subject of the verb to be mentioned.

    • jannikiluquruqa nimalu
    • ぢっにきをもるげにまを
    • janni-kiluqu⁠-ru⁠-qa nimalu
    • walk-ger-gen bear
    • “the bear’s walking”
      • guqqirupahi
    • ぐっねるぱひ
    • guqqi⁠-ru⁠-pahi
    • bad-ger-1gen
    • “my being bad”

This continues to apply in instances using argument promotion.

    • dasilluruqa dasu
    • だせっをるげだす
    • dasi⁠-illu⁠-ru⁠-qa dasu
    • eat-psv-ger-gen meat
    • “the eating of meat”
      • sikukkarudisi
    • せくっかるでせ
    • siku⁠-kka⁠-ru⁠-disi
    • die-abv-ger-3ani;gen
    • “his murdering of another”
      • ’usaxaruqa luna
    • うさざるげをな
    • ’usa⁠-xa⁠-ru⁠-qa luna
    • look-dtv-ger-gen moon
    • “looking at the moon”
