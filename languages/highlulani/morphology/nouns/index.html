Common Nouns Common nouns can refer to abstract or concrete items. lu’i ヲイ love bata バタ caution ’ahati アハテ happiness caga ヤガ mouth purissiji プリッセジ circle Stem Nouns Stem nouns are those that are neither compounded nor derived.

Mass Nouns

Mass nouns refer to an undifferentiated aggregation rather than separate units. These cannot take numbers, except for a postpended xita one to denote the smallest individual piece of that aggregate. ’iti イテ milk / ’itixita イテシタ drop of milk marru マッル flies / marruxita マッルシタ fly

Animacy

This determines which derivational suffixes can be applied, and which pronouns are used as reference.

Animate Noun

These refer to people and other multicellular organisms capable of independent movement. gusu グス person lulani ヲワニ queen kitisu キテス father sula スワ goat

Inanimate Noun

These refer to things such as natural phenomena, plants, fungi and unicellular life. tissa テッサ wind linu エヌ tree hurru ホッル yeast

Abstract Noun

This category encompasses concepts, ideas and other intangibles. They are referred to by inanimate pronouns. sutti スッテ fear ’adda アッダ past tuhhi トッヒ agriculture laru ワル year sajja サッヂ silence Compound Nouns The first part of a compound noun must be a noun, and the subsequent parts specify the meaning of that noun. These parts can be nouns or verbs. The whole noun is usually written without spaces. The syllabification and stress of nonce words are determined on a stem-by-stem basis. However, a compound in common use will start being treated whole. jifiru’inulli ジベルイヌッエ jifiru’i-nulli lake-mountain “mountain lake” xu’abaju ズアバヨ xu’a-baju bird-blue “bluebird” Proper Nouns Proper nouns begin with a capital letter in the transliteration. These are names that refer to individual people, places or things.
