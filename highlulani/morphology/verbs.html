Verbs

A verb is a necessary part of any sentence, except when making copular sentences.

Basic Verbs

The class of basic verbs is semi-closed. Basic verbs consist of one stem.

’i to say

dissu to feel

’usa to see

janni to move

kissa to fight

miku to be red

Compound Verbs

In order to extend or specify the meaning of many verbs, verbs can be compounded much as a noun can. Compound verbs are, however, much more common than compound nouns, to make up for the relative lack of basic verbs.

’isinna to tell a story (’i to speak + sinna story)

dissu’ahati to be happy (dissu to feel + ’ahati happiness)

pamilami to be conscious (pa think + milami surroundings)

Monosyllabic verbs may only be used this way. The object of an transitive monosyllabic verb can be used to form the compound, however, this does not occur with polysyllabic verbs.

dissu ’ahati to feel happiness (as above)

gasunu to put the cloak (somewhere) (ga to put + sunu cloak)

*ga sunu

*jannisunu

janni sunu to move the cloak (janni move)

Verbal Arguments

The arguments of a verb are those nouns which accompany it in order to construct a sentence. The two main arguments are the subject and the object, and verbs can be categorised by which of these are obligatory.

Intransitivity

Intransitive verbs only require a subject, not an object.

cura: to change

huba: to breathe

gupi: to wait

siku: to die

hacci: to be awake

nara: to sleep

Kinetic Verbs

These verbs deal with motion of the subject.

janni: to accelerate; to move around; to vibrate; to go; to come

’ussa: to follow

nura: to separate; to move away from someone else; to leave

madi: to rise; to be high

ka’u: jump

hussu: to drop; to fall; to be low

saja: lie down

tiku: turn; rotate

raca: to hang

fiqu: float; drift

Attributive Verbs

These verbs describe an attribute of the subject. With continuous auxiliaries,

mu: to be good

guqqi: to be bad

tuhi: to be little

ki: to be big

ri: to be smooth

qira: to be rough

nama: to be light

kuppu: to be strong

pani: to be tame

nittu: to be wild

runihi: to be similar to

tina: to differ from

suqa: to be ready

bi’u: to be sore

giri: to be pregnant

Chromatic Verbs

kiʔa: to be white; to turn white

ga: to be black; to turn black

miku: to be red; to make red

baju: to be blue; to turn blue

sa: to be yellow; to turn yellow

millu: to be brown; to turn brown
